# Sub-Project #9 Round-3 Sanity-Check Tools — Design Decision Research

**Date:** 2026-05-06
**Scope:** 8 design decisions for mean-reversion / volume-spike / divergence intraday-MIS detectors, benchmarked against Indian retail-platform pro-algo conventions (Streak, AlgoTest, uTrade, Tradejini, Goodwill, Share India, Samco, 5paisa, QuantInsti).
**Time-box:** 30 min wall-clock.

## Decisions Table

| # | Question | Indian-pro-algo convention | Sources | Recommended choice | One-line rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Entry: next-bar OPEN vs current-bar CLOSE? | Streak's documented model: signal evaluated at candle close; trade executed at **open of next candle** ("Signal candle = Trade candle - 1"). AlgoTest evaluates "Once Per Bar" at candle-close (59th sec) and triggers next bar. Industry-standard for look-ahead-free backtests. | [Streak Help — Signal/Trade candle](https://blog.streak.tech/help/what-is-signal-candle-trade-candle/), [AlgoTest — Candle Close Implementation](https://docs.algotest.in/product-blogs/candle-close-implementation-on-algotest/), [Streak Strategies Help](https://help.streak.tech/strategies/) | **Next bar OPEN** | Matches the dominant Indian retail backtest convention; eliminates look-ahead and aligns sanity-script results with what a Streak/AlgoTest user would see live. |
| 2 | Divergence return formulation: rolling N-bar vs since-today's-open? | No single dominant Indian convention. QuantInsti EPAT pairs work uses **rolling N-bar / closing-to-closing** (252d train, 21d test). Intraday divergence indicators on TradingView India typically use rolling lookback (e.g. 14, 30 bars). "Since-open" (anchored VWAP-style) is more common for VWAP-based intraday studies (Choice, Rupeezy, Goodwill). | [QuantInsti Cointegrated Pairs India](https://blog.quantinsti.com/cointegrated-pairs-trading-indian-equity-market-epat-project/), [Goodwill — VWAP Pro Tool](https://www.gwcindia.in/blog/vwap-volume-weighted-average-price-a-pro-tool-for-intraday-traders/), [Choice — VWAP Strategy](https://choiceindia.com/blog/vwap-trading-strategy) | **Rolling N-bar (e.g. 30×5m)** | Regime-stable, comparable across times of day, and matches stat-arb/pairs literature; "since-open" is too noisy in first 60 min and decays predictability after lunch. |
| 3 | Volume-spike: SHORT-only first or unified bidirectional? | No clear Indian-pro consensus. Ninja/Quantfish guidance: **separate long & short strategies preferred** because constraints differ (borrow, MIS short rules, asymmetric exhaustion dynamics). Zerodha Varsity treats blow-off-top (short) and capitulation-bottom (long) as **distinct patterns** — different volume signatures, different follow-through. | [Quantfish — Simultaneous vs Separate Long/Short](https://quant.fish/wiki/simultaneous-vs-separate-long-short-strategy-optimization/), [Zerodha Varsity — Volumes](https://zerodha.com/varsity/chapter/volumes/), [TradeFundrr — Volume Exhaustion](https://tradefundrr.com/volume-exhaustion-entry-setup/) | **SHORT-only first, then add LONG as separate detector** | Asymmetric mechanics (retail-loses thesis + short-side has cleaner exhaustion candles); validate edge in isolation before bolting on the long side. |
| 4 | Volume-spike T2 fallback at "bar midpoint" — published or invented? | **No published Indian or global precedent found** for "trigger-bar midpoint" as a time-stop fallback. Standard pro fallbacks are: (a) **time-stop close** (most common — Tradejini, Bracket-order convention), (b) **breakeven trail** (Share India, HDFC SEC TSL with book-profit), (c) **ATR-trail**. The "midpoint" idea appears as a **target** in spike patterns, not as a fallback exit. | [Tradejini — Bracket Orders](https://www.tradejini.com/blogs/intraday-trading-with-bracket-orders-on-tradejini-cubeplus), [HDFC Sec — TSL with Book Profit](https://www.hdfcsec.com/trailing-stop-loss-order-with-book-profit), [Altrady — Spike Pattern](https://www.altrady.com/crypto-trading/technical-analysis/spike-pattern) | **Time-stop CLOSE (exit at market on time-stop bar close)** | "Midpoint" is essentially a fabrication; time-stop-close is the documented, replicable convention used by Indian bracket-order frameworks. |
| 5 | NIFTY uptrend gate for LONG: 5m EMA20 vs 30-bar prior-close ROC? | Indian intraday VWAP/EMA blogs (Tradejini, Stockpathshala, Rupeezy, OnlineNIFM) overwhelmingly use **EMA on intraday timeframe** (9/20/21 EMA on 3m/5m) as the trend gate. ROC-style thresholds appear in academic papers, not in retail-pro intraday playbooks. | [Tradejini — Nifty Scalping Playbook](https://www.tradejini.com/blogs/a-technical-scalping-strategy-for-nifty-options), [Stockpathshala — VWAP+EMA](https://stockpathshala.com/vwap-and-ema-strategy/), [Rupeezy — VWAP Intraday](https://rupeezy.in/blog/vwap-trading-strategy-intraday-options) | **5m EMA20 (price > EMA20 AND EMA20 sloping up)** | Matches the dominant retail-pro convention; smoother than 30-bar ROC, easier to align with NIFTY scalping/VWAP playbooks the community already validates. |
| 6 | Beta: 30-day rolling daily, lagged 1 day vs bar-by-bar? | QuantInsti EPAT pairs work uses **OLS hedge ratio estimated on a training window (252d), held constant for the test step (21d)** — i.e. **lagged daily beta, applied to next period**. NSE/academic hedge-ratio research follows the same daily-OLS convention. Intraday bar-by-bar beta is rare in retail-pro Indian work due to noise. | [QuantInsti — Cointegrated Pairs India](https://blog.quantinsti.com/cointegrated-pairs-trading-indian-equity-market-epat-project/), [Medium — Dynamic Hedge Ratio Rolling Regression](https://medium.com/analytics-vidhya/dynamic-hedge-ratio-rolling-regression-for-pairs-trading-1befb0d86d38), [NSE — Optimal Hedge Ratio paper](https://nsearchives.nseindia.com/content/research/comppaper171.pdf) | **30-day rolling daily beta, lagged 1 day (uses up to D-1, applied on D)** | Standard academic + Indian-EPAT convention; lag eliminates look-ahead; daily beta is stable enough for intraday divergence at 5m without bar-level noise. |
| 7 | India VIX intraday: proxy from option IV or omit? | India VIX is itself **computed from NIFTY option bid-ask** (so an "IV proxy" is just rebuilding VIX). Indian retail-pro literature (5paisa, Samco, IFMC) treats VIX as a **daily/regime filter** — thresholds 15/18/20 quoted as daily values, not intraday. Intraday option-chain ATM IV is used by some option sellers but not as a VIX substitute for cash-segment mean-reversion gates. | [5paisa — Mean Reversion via VIX Extremes](https://www.5paisa.com/blog/mean-reversion-strategy-using-india-vix-extremes), [Samco — Volatility Trading](https://www.samco.in/knowledge-center/articles/volatility-trading-strategies-profiting-in-up-and-down/), [Zerodha Z-Connect — Trading India VIX](https://zerodha.com/z-connect/general/trading-india-vix-simplified) | **Use EOD VIX as a daily gate** (VIX-prev-close < threshold) | Convention is daily-only; an intraday IV-proxy adds engineering risk for marginal benefit. Override only if a future tick-VIX feed becomes available. |
| 8 | Tiered 50/50 (T1+T2) vs single exit at T2? | Indian retail-pro consensus favours **scaling out**: book partials at T1 (typically 50% qty), trail/hold remainder to T2. Tradejini, Share India, Samco, Stratzy all describe multi-target exits. Bracket-order frameworks default to single-target but practitioners explicitly recommend partials. Single-exit-at-T2 is acknowledged as simpler but lower realised win-rate. | [Tradejini — Risk Management Momentum](https://www.tradejini.com/blogs/importance-of-risk-management-in-momentum-trading-part-2), [Share India — Stop Loss Intraday](https://www.shareindia.com/knowledge-center/algo/what-is-stop-loss-in-intraday-trading), [Stratzy — Index Scalper Risk Mgmt](https://stratzy.in/blog/index-scalper-risk-management-system/) | **50/50 tiered (T1=1R, T2=2R) with breakeven trail after T1** | Matches dominant Indian-pro convention; raises hit-rate (T1 fills lock partial profit) and the breakeven-trail caps tail loss on the T2 leg. |
